Introducing the project

In 2020, the Abraham Accords were signed between Israel, the UAE and Bahrain, with Morocco and Sudan subsequently joining the agreement.

The Abraham Accords were the first time some signatory nations officially recognised Israel and established diplomatic relations with the country, enabling open trade and cooperation in various fields.

There are other Muslim-majority nations which do not recognise Israel officially but are expected to imminently normalise relations with Israel, including Saudi Arabia and Oman.

The aim of this project is to understand the factors which impact to the extent to which citizens of Muslim-majority countries are disposed to the normalization of ties with Israel.

The data

The most crucial data used in this project are the results of the 2022 Arab Opinion Index created by the Arab Center for Research and Policy Studies.

The parameter used from the index in the project is the percentage of respondents who answered "support" when asked support or oppose the diplomatic recognition of Israel by their country.

The index is based on the findings of a face-to-face survey conducted between June and December 2022 with 33,300 individual respondents in 14 Muslim-majority countries.

Additional important data sources used in the project include the World Bank and the United Nations.

The initial regressions

The PROFNI Index

Due to the relatively week r squared values found in my initial regressions, I devised an index called the Propensity for Normalisation with Israel (PROFNI) Index.

The index score for each country was calculated by adding the individual scores of 8 equally-weighted parameters, with 1 being the highest propensity for normalising relations with Israel and 0 being the lowest propensity.

The initial r squared of 0.13 found when regressing the PROFNI scores for all 14 survey countries is very low. However, after removing 4 outlier countries from the regression (Morocco, Qatar, Sudan and Algeria), the r squared for the 10 remaing countries was 0.87, indicative of a very strong positive correlation between the PROFNI Index score and support for diplomatic recognition of Israel.
